Our Holiday in Rome

In April 2023 we took a family holiday to Rome in Italy. It was the first time we had been there and we had a great time.

Travel

There were several stages to getting to our accomodation in central Rome. First we travelled by car from our home to Edinburgh Airport. We stayed in the longstay carpark which we have used before and find very simple and convenient.

Our flights were with Ryanair and this was the first time we had used them. Unfortunately there was a short delay due to strikes in France but otherwise the flight went well.

On arrival at Ciampino Airport we booked a coach to take us into Rome. Our accomodation was just a ten minute walk from the bus stop.

Accomodation

We stayed the week in The RomeHello Hostel which is located only ten minutes walk from Termini Station. We had a family room which had a double bed and bunk beds. The room was clean and comfortable.

The hostel had great facilities including:

  • Kitchen facilities
  • A range of entertainment including cookery courses
  • Shared common room space
  • Buffet breakfast (or breakfast boxes for those with early starts)

Activities

We had planned out our week in advance to make the best use of our time and had prebooked several tours and activities. Here are some of our favourites.

The Vatican

We knew from research that the Vatican can be very busy, particularly in Easter week. Therefore we had booked a tour through Made in Rome tours. This proved a great benefit as we were able to skip long queues and also were directed to the most significant parts of the vast museums. Seeing inside St Peter's Basilica was very interesting.

Colosseum

The Colosseum was the thing my son had most wanted to visit. It was very busy but we enjoyed seeing inside such an ancient building. We had audio visual guides which were helpful in explaining what we were seeing.

Stadio Domiziano

This is a less well know museum underneath Plaza Navona. It shows how the Plaza was once an athletics stadium and some of the original stadium has been excavated. My daughter particularly enjoyed the children's audio tour as it was entertaining for her age group.

Food and Drink

Rome is, of course, famous for food and drink and we tried to sample a range including:

  • Pizza
  • Lemoncello
  • Carbonara
  • Suppli

We were fortunate to attend two cooking classes in the hostel where we learned how to make carbonara and pizza. We joined in with cooking it and then ate the results. This was great fun and made a change from going out.

I loved trying new things, particularly suppli which are balls of fried rice filled with melted cheese - delicious!
